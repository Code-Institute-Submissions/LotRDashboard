  • LOTR Trilogy: Revenue: $2,917.5Bn / Budget: $281mil / Profit: $2,636.5Bn
  • The Hobbit Trilogy: Revenue: $2,935.4Bn / Budget: $667mil / Profit: 2,268.4Bn

We can discern from this data that a higher budget does not always equal a better performance in the Cinemas. The spread budget for the original trilogy is $187mil making that franchise far more profitable as the net profits head to $2,636.5Bn. Where as the Hobbit Trilogy budget comes in at $667mil so the net profit here is only $2,268.4Bn. What is surprising here is the fact in the original trilogy the creators had to literally invent new technology to be able to present many thousands of individuals on screen who were not actually there, this groundbroke CGI for years to come, especially in scenes from The Two Towers and the siege of Gondor in Return of the King. We could also safely believe that "Hype" was involved for the second trilogy, as seen in the movies reviews and academy wins.
